AI Receipt Intelligence

Beebag ¬∑ Malaysia ¬∑ FMCG Analytics

The Problem

FMCG partners needed spending data immediately. Processing thousands of receipts daily via manual entry was slow, expensive, and error-prone. Standard OCR failed on crumpled, stained, or non-standard receipts.

The System Architecture

  • Preprocessing: Computer vision for skew, contrast, noise correction
  • Extraction: OCR + NER to map unstructured text to structured JSON
  • Logic Layer: Deterministic validation (totals, dates, bounds)
  • Human-in-the-Loop: Confidence router with auto-approve thresholds

The Outcome

Processed 10,000+ receipts/day with minimal human intervention. Created a clean data lake enabling near real-time purchasing trend analysis, solving the "offline data gap."

AI Sales Suggestions

Lyncs Pulse ERP ¬∑ Saudi Arabia

The Problem

Sales teams had massive dashboards filled with numbers but lacked direction. They knew what happened, but not what to do next.

The Solution

  • Pattern Recognition: Analyzed historical purchase cycles to predict churn
  • Explainability: AI provided context ("Client X usually orders on Tuesdays")
  • Feedback Loop: "Was this helpful?" buttons adjusted model weights

The Impact

Transformed the ERP from a "System of Record" to a "System of Engagement." Sales teams gained actionable intelligence, not just historical data.

My Core Principles

Reliability > Novelty

A boring AI that works 99.9% of the time is infinitely more valuable than a brilliant AI that works 80% of the time. In Enterprise, trust is the only currency.

The Human-in-the-Loop Theorem

If Human Control is zero, the system value eventually collapses due to edge cases. I build systems where AI suggests, and Humans decide.

Scale Reveals Truth

You do not know if a system works until you load it with bad data, high traffic, and impatient users. I optimize for the "Worst Case Scenario," not the "Happy Path."

AI is a System, Not a Model

An LLM alone is not valuable. Value comes from the complete system: data pipelines, infrastructure, human workflows, and feedback loops.
